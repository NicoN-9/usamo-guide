## Overview

Geometric counting turns diagrams into countable structures. Frequent AMC 8
patterns include counting rectangles in a grid, squares in all sizes, and
lattice paths with restricted moves.

## Key Ideas

- Count by **size class** instead of one figure at a time.
- For grids, separate counting of squares and rectangles.
- Path counting often becomes a sequence of move choices.
- Use symmetry to halve computation when the diagram is balanced.

## Core Skills

### Counting Rectangles in an $m\times n$ Grid

Choose 2 of the $m+1$ horizontal lines and 2 of the $n+1$ vertical lines:

$$\binom{m+1}{2}\binom{n+1}{2}.$$

### Counting All Squares in an $n\times n$ Grid

Add contributions by side length:

$$1^2 + 2^2 + \cdots + n^2.$$

### Counting Monotone Paths

From $(0,0)$ to $(a,b)$ using only right/up moves, total paths are

$$\binom{a+b}{a} = \binom{a+b}{b}.$$

## Worked Example

How many rectangles are in a $2\times 3$ grid?

$$\binom{3}{2}\binom{4}{2}=3\cdot6=18.$$

So the grid contains $\boxed{18}$ rectangles.
