## Overview

3D geometry in foundations focuses on formulas and interpretation: identifying
faces, edges, and dimensions, then applying surface-area and volume models
correctly.

## Key Ideas

- Surface area adds the areas of all exposed faces.
- Volume measures 3D space and uses base-area-times-height structure for many
  solids.
- Nets help track adjacency and avoid counting hidden faces.
- Keep units consistent: area in square units, volume in cubic units.

## Core Skills

### Cube and Rectangular Prism

For side length $s$, cube formulas are:

- Surface area: $6s^2$
- Volume: $s^3$

For a prism with dimensions $l,w,h$:

- Surface area: $2(lw+lh+wh)$
- Volume: $lwh$

### Cylinder, Cone, Sphere

- Cylinder volume: $\pi r^2h$
- Cone volume: $\frac13\pi r^2h$
- Sphere volume: $\frac43\pi r^3$

### Similar Solids

If all linear dimensions scale by factor $k$, surface area scales by $k^2$ and
volume scales by $k^3$.

## Worked Example

A cube has side length 5. Find its surface area and volume.

Surface area $=6\cdot5^2=150$. Volume $=5^3=125$.

So the answers are $\boxed{150}$ and $\boxed{125}$.
