## Overview

A telescoping expression is designed so most terms cancel. This appears in AMC
style sequence and series questions where direct expansion would be too long.

## Key Ideas

- Rewrite terms to expose cancellation patterns.
- In telescoping sums, adjacent positive and negative parts cancel.
- In telescoping products, factors in numerator and denominator cancel.
- Write first few and last few terms before simplifying.

## Core Skills

### Telescoping Sum Pattern

A common form is

$$\sum_{k=1}^{n}(a_k-a_{k+1}) = a_1-a_{n+1}.$$

### Partial Fractions for Telescoping

Expressions like

$$\frac{1}{k(k+1)} = \frac{1}{k} - \frac{1}{k+1}$$

convert a hard sum into a telescoping sum.

### Telescoping Product Pattern

For products like

$$\prod_{k=1}^{n}\frac{k+1}{k}=\frac{2}{1}\cdot\frac{3}{2}\cdots\frac{n+1}{n}=n+1.$$

## Worked Example

Compute

$$\sum_{k=1}^{10}\frac{1}{k(k+1)}.$$

Rewrite each term as $\frac1k-\frac1{k+1}$:

$$\left(1-\frac12\right)+\left(\frac12-\frac13\right)+\cdots+\left(\frac1{10}-\frac1{11}\right)=1-\frac1{11}=\frac{10}{11}.$$

So the sum is $\boxed{\frac{10}{11}}$.
